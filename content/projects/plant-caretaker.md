context: >-
  Indoor plants die for a simple reason — nobody's home to water them, or
  they're watered too much. Holidays and busy weeks shouldn't cost you your
  plants.
problem: >-
  Build a hands-off system that keeps a plant correctly watered every day —
  never over- or under-watering — and tells you clearly when it needs a human.
solution:
  - Built an automatic irrigation system on Arduino that checks soil moisture daily.
  - When soil is wet enough, a green LED confirms a healthy, watered plant; when dry, the system waters the plant and plays a sound to confirm successful hydration.
  - A red LED alerts the user when the water reserve runs out — the only moment human attention is needed.
  - Designed the full build from lo-fi sketch to working circuit.
